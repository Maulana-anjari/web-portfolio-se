## Introduction

Technology is only as powerful as the community that understands and builds upon it. In March 2024, I had the honor of being a speaker at **ELI5: Blockchain Night Login**, an event organized by **Night Login DTETI FT UGM**—one of the most vibrant IT communities on our campus.

Alongside [Guntur Dharma Putra](https://gdputra.github.io), I spent the evening breaking down the complexities of blockchain technology for a room full of enthusiastic students. The goal was simple: "Explain It Like I'm 5" (ELI5), turning abstract cryptographic concepts into relatable, practical knowledge.

## Demystifying the Ledger

My talk focused on making blockchain approachable. Instead of diving straight into consensus algorithms, I started with the "Why": Why am I personally obsessed with this field compared to other IT disciplines?

The answer lies in **trust and transparency**. I discussed how blockchain isn't just about cryptocurrency, but about creating systems where security is baked into the architecture, not just added as an afterthought.

### Key Topics I Shared:

-   **The Basics**: What is a blockchain, and how does it differ from a traditional database?
-   **The Journey**: My personal experience in the Web3 ecosystem—what inspired me, the hurdles I faced, and how my research projects helped solidify my understanding.
-   **Future Potential**: How industries beyond finance (like healthcare and supply chain) are being revolutionized by transparent, immutable ledgers.
-   **Security and Efficiency**: Practical examples of how blockchain reduces friction and increases trust in digital interactions.

## Bridging Theory and Practice

One of the most rewarding parts of the session was the Q&A. Students were curious about the real-world application of the theories they learn in class. I was able to share specific insights from my work at Sumbu Labs and my internship at Kemdikbudristek, showing that blockchain is moving from experimental to enterprise-grade.
