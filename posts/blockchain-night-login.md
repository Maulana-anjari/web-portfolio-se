## Introduction

Technology is only as powerful as the community that understands and builds upon it. In March 2024, I had the honor of being a speaker at **ELI5: Blockchain Night Login**, an event that **Night Login DTETI FT UGM** organized. They are one of the most vibrant IT communities on our campus.

Alongside [Guntur Dharma Putra](https://gdputra.github.io), I spent the evening breaking down the complexities of blockchain technology for a room full of enthusiastic students. The goal was simple: "Explain It Like I'm 5" (ELI5), turning abstract cryptographic concepts into relatable, practical knowledge.

## Demystifying the Ledger

I focused my talk on making blockchain approachable. Instead of diving straight into consensus algorithms, I started with the "Why": explaining why I am obsessed with this field compared to other IT disciplines.

The answer is **trust and transparency**. Blockchain is about creating systems where security is inherent to the architecture, not added as an afterthought.

### Key Topics I Shared:

-   **The Basics**: Defining a blockchain and how it differs from a traditional database.
-   **The Journey**: My personal experience in the Web3 ecosystem, covering what inspired me and the hurdles I faced.
-   **Future Potential**: Transparent, immutable ledgers are revolutionizing industries beyond finance, including healthcare and supply chain.
-   **Security and Efficiency**: Practical examples of how blockchain reduces friction and increases trust in digital interactions.

## Bridging Theory and Practice

The Q&A was the most rewarding part. Students were curious about the real-world application of the theories they learn in class. I shared insights from my work at Sumbu Labs and my internship at Kemdikbudristek, showing that blockchain is moving from experimental to enterprise-grade.
